Tips for a Morning Routine That Supports Mental Health

A morning routine is a set of actions or activities that you do every morning to help you start your day on the right foot. Having a consistent morning routine can help you improve your mental health, increase productivity, and set a positive tone for the rest of the day.

There are many different types of morning routines, and what works for one person may not work for another. Some common elements of a morning routine include things like drinking water, stretching, meditating, journaling, or exercising. It's important to find a routine that works for you and that you enjoy, so that you're more likely to stick with it over time.

By establishing a healthy morning routine, you can improve your physical and mental well-being, boost your energy levels, and increase your overall sense of happiness and fulfillment. So if you're not already following a morning routine, it might be worth considering adding one to your daily routine.

Multiple studies have shown that establishing routines filled with healthy habits is a great way to move more efficiently through your day while expending less mental energy and even willpower in the process. A 2015 study on the psychology of habits demonstrated that people may rely more heavily on habits when stressed, suggesting that forming healthy routines could help people maintain physical, emotional, and mental health during stressful times.

HOW MUCH TIME DO YOU HAVE IN THE MORNING?

When building your morning routine, it's important to consider how much time you have. Even if you only have 20 minutes to spare each morning, you can use that time to promote good mental health.

If you have a very limited amount of time in the morning, try identifying your biggest pain points or stressors as you move through your morning rather than seeking to cram a variety of activities and tasks into a small window of time. Then consider how forming a habit could help you mitigate those pain points. For instance, someone who routinely doesn't have time for breakfast might plan and prepare their meals ahead of time so that a healthy option is always available to grab on their way out the door.

Having somewhere to be first thing in the morning doesn't have to mean limited time for a morning routine. If you are a morning person or prefer to rise at an earlier hour than most, you may have more time to dedicate to a morning routine.

Those who have close to an hour or longer to dedicate to a morning routine might take a different route when choosing which habits will best support their well-being throughout the rest of the day. Someone might decide to dedicate more time to a physical activity and go for a walk or run, take an exercise class, or practice yoga. Spending more time on meditation, planning the rest of the day, or preparing a healthful meal might also be easier to accommodate with this schedule.

The Benefits of Establishing a Consistent Morning Routine

  1. A morning routine can help reduce stress and anxiety by providing a sense of structure and predictability to your day.
  2. By starting your day with healthy habits like exercise or healthy eating, you can set the tone for a healthier lifestyle overall.
  3. A morning routine can help you prioritize your time and ensure that you're focusing on the things that are most important to you.
  4. By taking time in the morning to reflect on your goals and intentions for the day, you can feel more focused and motivated throughout the day.
  5. Having a consistent morning routine can help you establish a sense of self-discipline and control over your life, which can lead to increased feelings of confidence and empowerment.
  6. A morning routine can also provide a sense of self-care and self-love, as you prioritize taking care of yourself first thing in the morning.

Overall, a morning routine can have many benefits for your physical and mental health, and can help you create a more fulfilling and productive life. So if you're not already following a morning routine, it might be worth considering adding one to your daily routine.

Routines

Let light in

Exposure to bright light first thing in the morning increases feelings of wakefulness. To clear away morning grogginess, take in some sunlight within the first 5 to 10 minutes of waking up in the morning. Exposure to sunlight can have a positive impact on mental health by regulating sleep, and reducing symptoms of anxiety and depression.

Make your bed

Stick to a consistent sleep schedule, create a relaxing bedtime routine, and make your bedroom a sleep-friendly environment. Avoid screens, caffeine, and alcohol before bed, and invest in comfortable bedding. By getting enough rest, the body is better equipped to manage stress, which can have a positive impact on mental health.

Hydrate

Drink a glass of water to rehydrate after a night's sleep and jumpstart your metabolism. Drinking water can also help regulate the body's sleep-wake cycle, which in turn can improve mood and reduce symptoms of depression. Overall, staying hydrated is an important part of maintaining good mental health and well-being.

Nourish

Nourishing the body with a balanced and nutritious diet can have several positive effects on mental health. Eating a diet rich in whole foods, such as fruits, vegetables, whole grains, and lean proteins, can provide the body with essential nutrients that are important for brain function, including memory and focus. Finally, eating a healthy diet can improve gut health, which has been linked to better mental health outcomes.

Motivate

Feeling motivated can have several positive effects on mental health. Having a sense of purpose and direction can provide a sense of fulfillment and satisfaction, which can improve overall well-being. Having a positive outlook and feeling motivated can improve social connections and relationships, which are important for maintaining good mental health. It helps by reducing symptoms of anxiety and depression, and promoting overall well-being.

Avoid technology

Avoiding technology, or at least reducing its use, can have several positive effects on mental health. Social media and other digital platforms can contribute to feelings of isolation, comparison, and low self-esteem, which can lead to symptoms of anxiety and depression. Reducing technology use can encourage individuals to engage in other activities, such as exercise, socializing, and hobbies, which have been linked to better mental health outcomes.

Meditate

Meditation is a practice that involves focusing the mind on a particular object or thought to achieve a state of mental clarity and emotional calmness. Regular meditation practice has been shown to have several benefits for mental health, including reducing stress and anxiety, enhancing attention and focus, decreasing symptoms of depression, and boosting cognitive function.

Make a list

By doing this, individuals can prioritize their tasks and tackle them in a structured and organized manner, which can help to reduce stress and improve feelings of control and achievement. It can also help to increase productivity, which can have a positive impact on self-esteem and confidence. It can help to improve mental health by improving focus and productivity.

Physical activity

Regular exercise can help reduce stress and anxiety by releasing endorphins, the brain's natural mood-boosting chemicals. It has also been linked to improved mood and a reduction in symptoms of depression. It help individuals develop healthy coping mechanisms. Physical activity can improve cognitive function, including memory and attention, and promote better sleep quality.
